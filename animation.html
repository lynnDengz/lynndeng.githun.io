Animation

My animation project, “The Orphan of Zhao,” is a retelling of a classic Chinese story that explores themes of revenge, sacrifice, and redemption. Through the use of animation, I aimed to bring this ancient tale to life in a modern context, making it accessible to audiences of all ages and backgrounds. In creating this project, I drew inspiration from a variety of sources, including traditional Chinese art and storytelling techniques, as well as contemporary animation styles. I paid particular attention to the details of character design, movement, and composition, seeking to create a rich and immersive world that would captivate viewers and draw them into the story. Ultimately, my goal with “The Orphan of Zhao” was to create a work of art that would not only entertain and educate, but also inspire reflection and discussion about the universal human experiences that the story portrays. Through the use of animation, I believe that I was able to convey the emotional depth and complexity of the characters and their relationships, inviting viewers to connect with them on a deeper level and engage with the story in a meaningful way.
